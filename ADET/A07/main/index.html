Original Pilipino Music
Original Pilipino Music (OPM) is music created by Filipino artists. It reflects the heart, culture, and everyday stories of the Filipino people.
From love songs to upbeat tunes, OPM connects people through songs that are truly Filipino.

History of OPM

Explore how Filipino music has evolved over the years.

1940s–1960s: The Early Influences

Filipino music during this time was heavily influenced by American jazz, swing, and kundiman. Local artists began blending Western sounds with Filipino themes and language.

1970s: The Birth of OPM

The term "Original Pilipino Music" or OPM emerged in the 1970s as artists like Freddie Aguilar and Hotdog started creating original songs in Filipino languages. This era marked the rise of Manila Sound.

1980s–1990s: OPM Boom

OPM gained massive popularity with bands and solo artists dominating radio and television. The music tackled themes of love, patriotism, and social issues, making it relatable to the masses.

2000s–Present: Revival and Innovation

Modern OPM artists fuse pop, R&B, hip-hop, indie, and electronic styles. Acts like Ben&Ben, Moira Dela Torre, and SB19 represent the genre's evolution and global reach.
